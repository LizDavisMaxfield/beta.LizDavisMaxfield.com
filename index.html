about

Liz Davis Maxfield is "a rising star in the Celtic music scene and she is revolutionizing the use of cello in performance." (Natalie Haas).

An eclectic cellist with extensive experience as a performer, recording artist, and teacher, Liz started playing the cello at the age of ten and she recorded her first album before she turned twelve. Since that time, Liz has recorded ten albums and has been featured on dozens more.

Soon after graduating from Berklee College of Music, she was awarded a Fulbright to study towards a MA in Irish Traditional Music Performance at the University of Limerick. Her book, "The Irish Cello Book: Traditional Tunes and Techniques" published by Berklee Press and Hal Leonard has garnered positive reviews and has been dubbed "The definitive classic for cellists looking to play Irish music in an authentic and meaningful way." (Mike Block).

Liz has performed across the globe as a solo artist and with various groups including New Time Ensemble, Folk Arts Quartet, and Ampersand. Her compositions for cello choir are enjoyed by a wide range of cellists, and she teaches folk and alternative cello styles privately, via Skype, and in workshops throughout the nation.

She lives in Provo, Utah with her husband, Andrew Maxfield, and her sons, Eliot and Simon. She plays a 2007 J.P. Lucas cello.

books

This book will soon be seen as the definitive classic for cellists looking to play Irish music in an authentic and meaningful way."

- Mike Block, Multi-style cellist and director of Mike Block String Camps

The Irish Cello Book introduces cellists to ornamentation, bowing, and other stylistic attributes of Irish music. Published by Hal Leonard/Berklee Press, the book includes over 100 tunes, straightforward lessons, and a reference CD featuring artists such as Liz Carroll, Siobhan Peoples, Matt Crannitch, and more.

Each copy of The Irish Cello Book comes with a free Skype cello lesson with Liz. Order your copy today!

sheetmusic

I am first and foremost a tunesmith, but I also enjoy arranging and composing for string players. My compositions have been enjoyed by cellists and other string players across the country. I always include performance notes to guide players through non-classical or unconventional techniques. And I'm available for lessons and workshops (in person or via Skype) to answer any questions.
